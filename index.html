My design process

Discovery.

This is how I call our first interaction. I'll meet you and understand who you are as a person and a professional, and you get to know me as a human and a professional. This is a crucial point. We will not go forward with the project if I feel like our values and beliefs do not match, or if we cannot understand each others goals.

At this point I want to know evertyhing. Where you come from, who you are, what do you want, your goals, your mission, your vision and values. Who is your target, what you expect to take from this project... These are the core of the project and the true reason why we will move forward to the next part of this journey.

This is also the part where the research starts - once I gather all the info I can from you, I will sit down and understand your niche, your clients and your competitors. Finding out who is the top player in your game gives us an advantage, and pushes us in the right direction, which is towards creating a beautiful piece of work that helps you achieve your goals and spread your passion.

Creation.

This is a broad process - probably the one we will spend most time on. Even though the research part also counts as a creation, for that it is a hugely important part of what's the outcome look like, I classify as creation anything that will help us during our journey. From discovering which tools work best for your project, going through the testing these tools, to the wireframing, prototyping and developing part. Creation brings it all together.

That's when the magic happens. Seek, create, impress. Your opinion will be greatly valued during the whole process, and we will, together, build a beautiful, functional and efficient website, so you will have happy and returning customers.

Delivery.

It's ready, we've go a product. This is the moment of test, launch and follow-up. It's the post-production and it's when we put at proof all the concepts developed in the creation process.

Once the website is launched, there's still a careful and meticulous process of checking up and trying to figure out weak spots and where we can improve, followed by lots of hard work.

More than clients, I like to make friends and partners. That's the main reason I put so much care and jump into every project as it was my last one. The difference between someone like me and most of the others out there is simple - I genuinely give a shit.
